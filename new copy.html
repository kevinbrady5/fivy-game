How To Play

The goal of Fivey is to get rid of all your cards before the end of the round.

  • Fivey is played in rounds
  • Each round has 5 turns
  • The game starts with 5 face down cards lined up in the middle of the table, these are the turn cards
  • Each player is dealt 5 cards
  • The remaining cards create a face down draw pile
  • At the start of each turn, the corresponding turn card is turned face up
  • You get rid of your cards by playing them when it is your turn
  • You play cards by adding them on to the face up turn cards
  • You can add cards to count up or down on any face up turn card if they are the same suit
  • Or you can play a card of any suit on a face up turn card if they have matching values
  • A player's turn begins by drawing 1 card from the draw pile
  • The player can then play any number of cards until they have no playable cards left
  • This ends the player's turn and play continues with the next player
  • Each turn has a random turn modifier
  • The turn modifier will give instructions that all players follow before the turn starts or it will specify a modifier that lasts for that turn only

Here is an example of gameplay:

The game is setup by shuffling the deck, laying down the 5 face down turn cards, dealing 5 cards to each player, and placing the remaining cards face down as a draw pile

  1. The 1st turn begins by turning over the 1st turn card and following the turn modifier instructions
  2. The 1st player draws 1 card and plays any playable cards in their hand
  3. Then the next player does the same
  4. After the last player's turn, the 1st turn is over
  5. The 2nd turn begins by turning over the 2nd turn card and following the turn instructions
  6. Play continues the same as the 1st turn except now the players have 2 turn cards to play on and a different turn modififer
  7. Play continues like this until the end of the 5th turn, then the round is finished
  8. After each round, the score is updated
  9. After the final round, the game is over!

Some other special rules to note...

  • During a player's turn, if there is only one face up turn card in a pile, they can play it on another face up turn card where applicable. This creates a free space to play any card the player chooses
  • Even if a player gets rid of all their cards before the end of the round, they can still be dealt back in by a turn modifier that instructs all players to draw cards or pass cards to another player
